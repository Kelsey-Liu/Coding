Data Dictionary

  1. survival Survival: 0 = No, 1 = Yes
  2. pclass: Ticket class 1 = 1st, 2 = 2nd, 3 = 3rd
  3. sex: Sex
  4. Age: Age in years
  5. sibsp: # of siblings / spouses aboard the Titanic
  6. parch: # of parents / children aboard the Titanic
  7. ticket: Ticket number
  8. fare: Passenger fare
  9. cabin: Cabin number
  10. embarked: Port of Embarkation C = Cherbourg, Q = Queenstown, S = Southampton

Interpretation:¶

When comparing the Accuracy among all 9 models, we can see that 4 of them have quite similar performance, among which I took top 2, "RandomForest" and "GradientBoosting", for further comparison. As seen in the confusion matrix, although the difference is very little, Random Forest did better in predicting the "actual survived" group, resulting in higher recall and lower precision; on the other hand, Decion Tree did better in predicting the "actula not survived" group, resulting in lower recall and higher precision. Overall, Random Forest scores a little bit higher in f-1 score than Decision Tree, and since the f-1 score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, which is a better measure than accuracy, I choose the Random Forest in predicting the holdout test data. In reality, whether to choose the one model with better precision or with better recall can depend on which label we care about more (risk on which wrongly predicted label group is what we're more willing to take.)
