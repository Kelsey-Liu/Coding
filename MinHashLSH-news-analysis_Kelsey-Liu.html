Assignment 9¶

Kelsey Liu

You have ~100K news articles loaded into GCS. You can access the data one of the following ways:

  1. Reading into Spark DF from GCP spark.read.parquet (gs://msca-bdp-data-open/news_similar/) - preferred
  2. Download / transfer a single parquet file into alternative location
    https://storage.googleapis.com/msca-bdp-data-open/news/news_similar.parquetLinks.

Your goal is to determine how many of these English articles are unique vs. how many are “near-duplicate”.

  1. You can run this assignment on the infrastructure of your choice (GCP, Colab, etc.)
  2. To assess the similarity, you need to run it on both title and text variables using MinHashLSH from pyspark.ml.feature
  3. Identify the right Jaccard similarity for this corpus of text and explain how you selected it
  4. Provide a recommendation whether you should be using title, text or both for “near-duplicate” detection mechanism
  5. Build a bar-chart visualization for two variables (count of unique and count of “near-duplicate” articles)
  6. Build a histogram showing the overall distribution of “near-duplication”
  7. You must build your charts directly in Jupyter Notebook
  8. The assignment must be submitted as Jupyter Notebook (as both an ipynb file and a html/pdf file.)

5. Insights and Interpretation¶

Similarity Evaluation:¶

  • 【Title】: Based on how titles look like around each threshold, we can evaluate if that threshold is appropriate for our use case: for the low (0.3) Jaccard distance , the content is near identical except the source; We can observe the similar thing happens with threshold being set to medium (0.5) Jaccard distance, but instead of just source being different, some of them seem to have high similarity because they borrowed video title as huge part of the news title; As for the high (0.7) Jaccard distance, the difference between two pieces of news articles is quite obvious just by looking at the title itself. If we take another look at the random sample of records comparing side-by-side across 3 thresholds, it becomes even clearer that starting from 0.5 threshold, the content might be about the same topic but it actually differs slightly from each other.

  • 【Text】: Similar to Title, we can observe that the lower the threshold, the more similar two pieces of news articles are. At low (0.3) Jaccard distance, we can already see some differences in text around the threshold, and it's reasonable to suspect that, although words are differently ordered and some content articles are mixed together, they are still under the influence of each other, meaning they can be considered as "duplicate". However, as the Jaccard distance goes up, the difference becomes bigger and shouldn't be considered as duplicate articles.

Conclusion:¶

  • Variable: Since the text also includes the title, and text provides extra information on the article for us to analyze, it will be better and more accurate to run MinHashLSH on text instead of title (not both either, since most text already contains title). In addition, if some articles are using wording borrowed from a certain object as their title (ex. video title), it is possible the titles from two complete different content articles end up being very similar to each other. Therefore, we should always run the analysis on text instead of title to assess their similarity.

  • Jaccard similarity: should be set at 0.3 (on text).

Challenges:¶

  • Some news might have very similar structure already written out, with different topic they just replace certain words with very little change, which will be deemed as duplicate in our analysis, but the topic is totally different. The most common case is the tiktok related news title with the hashtag used to refer to the video title, which will need further cleaning or processing based on different use case.
